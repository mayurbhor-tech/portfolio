Dev Insights

// My perspective on tech, growth, and the craft of building

⚡ On AI/ML in 2025
We're past the hype cycle. Developers who will stand out go beyond calling APIs — understanding model architecture, fine-tuning, and prompt engineering as first-class disciplines. FastAPI + Python + a well-structured ML pipeline is one of the most employable tech stacks right now.
⚙ On Backend Engineering
FastAPI is genuinely excellent — async-first, auto-docs, Pydantic validation. But the real power comes pairing it with Docker and deploying on AWS EC2. Learning infrastructure early, not just code, makes you 10x more valuable than developers who only know the framework.
◉ On Learning as a Student
The gap between what colleges teach and what industry needs is real, but closeable. Every internship taught me more in 2 months than a semester of theory. Build things, break things, ship publicly. GitHub is your real resume — make it count.
⬡ On Cloud & DevOps
AWS changed how I think about software permanently. Going from a local script to serving at scale behind EC2 with S3 storage shifts your mental model entirely. Even basic cloud fluency — VPCs, IAM, S3 policies — makes you a much stronger developer at any level.
◐ On Full Stack
Full stack doesn't mean mediocre at everything. It means owning a feature end-to-end — design the API, wire the React frontend, handle the DB schema, deploy it. That end-to-end ownership mindset is what fast-moving teams desperately need.
▲ The Road Ahead
AI + Cloud + Scalable Backend is the triangle that will define software engineering for the next decade. I'm positioning myself at that intersection intentionally — building genuine depth in Python, ML pipelines, and distributed systems while the field is still young enough to shape.
